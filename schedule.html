Chasing Cyber's Tail

9:00am Room 1, Nicholas Carroll

In "Chasing Cyber's Tail" we'll discuss the reason some organizations constantly find themselves behind attackers in their environment by constantly chasing the latest IOCs and how leaders can adapt their current threat intelligence focuses to cast a better net for attacker behavior in their environment, giving their team a chance to head attackers off. We'll investigate how summiting even one or two rungs of the Pyramid of Pain can pay dividends for creating a proactive hunting program and look at real world examples from ransomware groups for the difference in outcomes that follows shifting focusing on IOCs and TTPs in an environment.

TTP Pyramid

10:00am Main Room, Christopher Peacock

This talk is geared to those seeking to make Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI) actionable through emulation and detection engineering. In the discussion, we will cover the expansion of David Bianco's Pyramid of Pain, the Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures (TTP) Pyramid. We will cover a brief history of cyber intelligence, its evolution, and how MITRE ATT&CK fits in. We then will show what each level is representative of and how it can be leveraged. Finally, we will highlight what getting to the procedure level means, along with how to use procedural level intelligence in an actionable manner effectively.

Threat Hunting In Practice

10:00am Room 1, Michael-Angelo Zummo

Today’s organizations face unprecedented challenges in battling cyber threats. Between increasingly sophisticated cybercriminals, rapidly expanding digital assets and attack surfaces, and a legal landscape that threatens to punish those companies that fail to adequately protect their customers’ privacy rights, it’s not hard to see why cybersecurity professionals are buckling under the mounting pressure. Within this complex new cyber threat battleground, organizations cannot afford to continue relying on passive, reactive defense, and instead, must leverage the tools and methodologies to facilitate a truly proactive and preemptive cyber defensive program. Threat hunting is a powerful tool enabling companies to take a proactive approach to build organizational cyber resilience. By seeking out the highest-priority potential cyber threats rather than waiting for evidence of them to appear, threat hunting provides a proven, systematic way for an organization to defend itself from the most concerning risks, reducing dwell times and accelerating remediation and response. This session is an in-depth technical demonstration of the threat hunting process on the deep and dark web which will demonstrate new techniques and innovative threat hunting tools that empower organizations to proactively block threats before they can materialize into catastrophic cyber incidents. Michael-Angelo Zummo, Senior Threat Intel Specialist at Cybersixgill, will demonstrate the best practices for performing an effective threat hunt.

The Cyber Immune System - A Mental Model

10:00am Room 2, Will Robus

Much like the human body, organizations are complex systems that live and breathe. In order to stay healthy and at our full potential, our bodies have evolved a robust and some-times anti-fragile immune system. The system uses filtration, identification of invaders, anti-bodies, T-cells, white-blood cells, and even blood clotting. These tools work in harmonic unison to defend against outside invaders getting access to our core systems, as well as search and destroy anything inside of our bodies that is meant to do us harm. As security practitioners and security leaders, it is our sole purpose to ensure the immune system of our organizations are operating at the highest possible level. It is not a question of will a cyberthreat become viable inside of the environment you protect, but when. Your ultimate success is directly tied to the short term and long term health of the security "immune" systems in place to keep your organization safe from harm. In this talk we'll explore the parallels between the human immune system and cybersecurity threat detection/response systems - with the goal of giving you a new way to look at the future of cybersecurity inside of your organization.

Mind the Gap: Becoming a Product Manager in InfoSec

2:00pm Room 1, Daniel Fernandez

Externally, information security can be perceived as exclusively about penetration testing, threat hunting, incident response, or security policy. However, there are countless possible careers, many of which remain unfilled. As security goes from a compliance requirement to a competitive advantage, enterprise software companies and their customers must consider their security as a product. Prioritizing what is most valuable to customers given resource constraints and thinking about security strategically are some of the elements a product mindset brings. Even if organizations buy vs. build, they must ensure all the pieces work together. Transitioning to security as an existing product manager or becoming a product manager focused on security takes some adjustment. Essential skills are needed to be a product manager, which can be relevant across many other professions. With those skills and guidance on navigating the information security industry, many professionals can make the transition and bridge this less known talent gap.
